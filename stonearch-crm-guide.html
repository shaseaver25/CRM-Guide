Welcome & orientation

cM CRM · crm.stonearch.ai

Welcome to your CRM

A plain-English guide to running your business out of one system — your people, your money, your work, and your outreach, all in one place.

What this is. A CRM (Customer Relationship Management) is one organized home for everyone you do business with and everything tied to them: what they've bought, what you're working on, and what you send them. Instead of scattered spreadsheets and inboxes, it all lives here. This build also keeps a light Grants thread for the grant work you manage.

The five ideas the whole system runs on

MemberA person you do business with — a customer, prospect, or supporter. Lives in Members, each with a full history.
Payment & CommitmentA payment is money received. A commitment is money promised for later. You record both on a member's profile.
Deal / Sales PipelineA sale you're working toward, moving through stages from first contact to closed. Lives in Sales Pipeline.
Company & Member ClientCompanies = orgs you're courting (a BD pipeline). Member Clients (in Admin) = accounts set up on the CRM.

How the menu is organized

  • Main — people and money: Dashboard, Members, Contacts, Sales Pipeline, Companies.
  • Work — getting things done: Calendar, Projects, Tasks, Voice Notes.
  • Events & Programs — Events you host and registrations.
  • Marketing — Email Marketing campaigns and the Content Repository.
  • Finance — Payments and Financial Runway.
  • Admin — Member Clients, Integrations, and system settings (for owners/admins).

Start here · Dashboard

Dashboard

Your home base — the first screen after you log in. It answers one question: "What needs my attention right now?"

  1. Quick actions (top row) — one-click shortcuts to the things you do most: add a member, start a new deal, send email, open your calendar or projects.
  2. My Tasks — everything assigned to you across the whole system, in one spot. "All caught up" means nothing's waiting.
  3. cM Customers — a quick count of member-client accounts that are Active vs. Pending setup.
  4. Recent Activity — a live feed of what changed: new members added, deal status changes. Your "what did I miss?" glance.
  5. Stripe (bottom-right card) — connect Stripe so online payments and subscriptions flow into the CRM automatically. Ask an admin before connecting.
Daily habit: read Recent Activity top-to-bottom, then check My Tasks. Fifteen seconds tells you what needs you today.
People & money · Members

Members

Your customer database — the heart of the CRM. Every customer, prospect, and supporter is a card here, and clicking one opens their full history.

  1. Add Member (top-right) — create one new person by hand. The grid/list toggle beside it switches between picture cards and a compact table.
  2. Bulk Import & Clean Emails — import many members at once from a spreadsheet, and run a cleanup that flags bad or duplicate email addresses.
  3. Lapsed Members filter — flip it on to instantly show people who bought last year but nothing yet this year: your prime win-back list.
  4. Lifetime Value (on each card) — the total a member has ever paid you. The quickest read on how significant a relationship is.

The 360° Customer View

Click any card to open the full profile in a pop-up. This is where you'll spend most of your time with an individual customer.

  1. Log Interaction — record a call, meeting, or email so the relationship history stays complete.
  2. Contact Information — email (with a Send Email button) and mailing address.
  3. Payment History — total revenue plus three actions: Record Payment (money received), Record Commitment (money promised), and Track Major Deal (start a pipeline opportunity).
  4. Linked Grants, Relationships, Related Tasks — connect the person to a grant, link them to a company or another member, or create a follow-up to-do tied to them.

Try it: record a payment

  1. Click a member's card to open the 360° view.
  2. Scroll to Payment History and click Record Payment.
  3. Enter the amount and date, then save — watch Lifetime Value update.
  4. Optional: click Log Interaction to note the thank-you call you'll make.
Members vs. Contacts: Members are the people money flows from (customers, with payment histories). Contacts is a simpler directory of everyone else — vendors, partners, advisors. Next section.
People & money · Contacts

Contacts

A straightforward address book of everyone who isn't a customer — vendors, partners, advisors, lobbyists. No payment history, just who they are and how to reach them.

  1. Search — find anyone by name, email, or job title.
  2. Type filter — narrow the list to a category of contact.
  3. Type column — labels each record (e.g. "General Contact") so you can tell roles apart at a glance.
Rule of thumb: if money flows from this person, they belong in Members. If they're a professional relationship, they belong here in Contacts.
People & money · Sales Pipeline

Sales Pipeline

A health dashboard for the deals you're working toward. It shows how much money is "in flight," how likely it is to land, and where deals get stuck.

  1. Three tabs — Sales Pipeline (deals), Programs (program health), and Client Engagements.
  2. Customer Lifetime Value — money realized plus what's still in the pipeline. Pipeline Conversion — the share of deals that turn into revenue.
  3. Customer Acquisition Cost — roughly what it costs to land each deal; an efficiency check.
  4. Average Deal Size & Total Opportunities — plus, lower down, stage distribution and insights that flag stages converting below 30% as bottlenecks.
How a deal gets here: open a member's 360° view and click Track Major Deal. That creates a pipeline opportunity that moves through stages (prospecting → qualifying → proposal → closed) and shows up on this dashboard. "Weighted" value = the dollar amount × its probability of closing.
People & money · Companies

Companies

A pipeline of organizations you're courting as partners or accounts — separate from individual customers. Track each one from first contact through to active partner.

  1. Add Company — add a new organization to the pipeline.
  2. KPIs — Total Companies, Prospects, In Pipeline, Active Partners at a glance.
  3. Stages — Prospects → Contacted → In Conversation → Pilot → Active Partners. Drag a card along as the relationship progresses.
  4. Pipeline / Kanban / List (top-right) — three ways to view the same companies.
Companies vs. Member Clients: Companies are organizations you're pursuing. Member Clients (in the Admin menu) are accounts already set up on the CRM. Different jobs — see the Admin section.
Getting work done · Calendar

Calendar

Connects your Microsoft 365 calendar so your meetings live alongside your customer work — and helps you prep for them.

  1. Two tabs — Upcoming Meetings (your synced events) and Settings (sync preferences).
  2. Connect Calendar — links Microsoft 365 once. After that, meetings appear automatically and you can attach prep tasks to each.
Why connect: once synced, a "meeting with a customer" turns into linked prep work and follow-ups without re-typing anything. Check with your admin about which account to connect.
Getting work done · Projects

Projects

Visual "boards" for managing work — like sticky notes on a wall that you drag from one column to the next as work progresses. Great for launches, grant applications, and team initiatives.

  1. New Board / From Template — start a board from scratch or from a ready-made template.
  2. Filters — narrow to My Boards, Ideas, or Archived so the list stays manageable.
  3. Board cards — each shows its card count, members, and a type label. A board can be Linked to a grant or deal so the work and the money stay connected.

Inside a board (the Kanban view)

  1. Columns = stages. Work flows left to right (Done → In Progress → This Week → Next 2 Weeks). "P0" means top priority, "P1" next. Drag a card to a new column as it progresses.
  2. Cards = tasks. Each has a title, a short note tagged by area, and a priority badge.
  3. AI helpers — Parse Agenda turns a pasted meeting agenda into cards automatically; AI Suggest proposes next-step cards. Big time-savers.
Getting work done · Tasks

Tasks

One unified to-do list that gathers tasks from everywhere in the system — projects, members, voice notes — so nothing slips through the cracks.

  1. Three views — My Tasks (just yours), All Tasks (the whole team's), and ROI Dashboard (which work is paying off).
  2. Sync All Sources — pull in the latest tasks from projects, members, and other modules so the list is current.
  3. Estimate ROI — an AI helper that gauges a task's likely payoff, so you can prioritize the high-value work.
Tasks vs. Projects: Projects show work visually on a board; Tasks is the flat checklist pulled together from all those boards and modules. Projects to organize, Tasks to make sure nothing is forgotten.
Getting work done · Voice Notes

Voice Notes

Record or upload a meeting and the system transcribes it, then automatically pulls out new contacts and follow-up steps. Perfect after a networking event or customer lunch.

  1. New Recording / Upload Meeting — record live in the browser, or upload an audio/video file of a meeting you already had.
  2. Stat cards — recordings made, new contacts it created, follow-ups still pending, and notes added.
  3. Tabs — read the Transcripts, work your auto-generated Next Steps, and see what teammates captured.
The magic: after a recording, the system suggests new contacts mentioned and a list of follow-up tasks — so a 30-minute coffee chat becomes clean records and to-dos with no manual typing.
Outreach · Events

Events

Create and manage your events — workshops, webinars, open houses — and track who registers.

  1. Create Event — opens a form for name, type, date/time, location (in-person / virtual / hybrid), and venue.
  2. Search & status filter — find an event and filter by where it stands (upcoming, past, etc.).
Connects to your members: registrations tie back to member records, so you can see who attended and follow up — and pair events with an Email Marketing invite.
Outreach · Email Marketing

Email Marketing

Send email campaigns to your contacts and see how they perform — opens, clicks, deliveries — all in one place.

  1. New Campaign — build and send a new email to a chosen group.
  2. Performance metrics — Delivered, Average Open Rate, and Average Click Rate tell you whether people actually read it.
  3. Status tabs — follow a campaign through its life: Draft → Scheduled → Sending → Sent.
Before a real send: double-check your recipient list and have someone proof the draft. A campaign goes to many people at once and can't be un-sent.
Outreach · Content Repository

Content Repository

A reusable library of writing for proposals and grant applications — mission statements, budgets, impact narratives. Stop rewriting the same paragraphs; find the best version and copy it.

  1. Add Content — save a new reusable snippet, optionally marking it a Template.
  2. Semantic Search — search by meaning, not just exact words. Type "how we help customers" and it finds the right paragraph even if those words aren't in it.
  3. Copy & usage count — one click copies the text to paste into a proposal; "Used 15 times" shows which snippets are proven winners.
Workflow: writing a proposal or grant? Come here first. Search the section you need, copy the strongest version, tailor it, and submit. The usage counts steer you to what's worked before.
Admin & reference · Admin

Admin & Member Clients

The owner/admin area — manage users, connect tools, and handle the accounts set up on the CRM. Most day-to-day users won't need this; it sits at the bottom of the menu.

  1. Admin Dashboard — users, roles, and system settings. Quick actions to add a user, manage roles & permissions, and review escalations.
  2. Member Clients — the accounts set up on the CRM, with their tier, status, and setup progress. (Different from Companies, which is your BD pipeline.)
  3. Integrations — connect outside tools, including Stripe for payments.
  4. Finance (Payments, Financial Runway) — sits just above Admin for money-in and cash-runway views.

Settings & Help

At the very bottom of the menu: Settings (starting with Change Password), Help for support, and Sign Out. Use Sign Out on shared computers.

Ask an admin, don't do solo: connecting integrations (Stripe, Microsoft 365), bulk-importing members, changing permissions, or sending a live email campaign. Most actions are reversible — sends and deletes are not.
Admin & reference · Daily workflow

Your everyday workflow

Once the pieces make sense, most days follow a simple rhythm.

A typical morning (5 minutes)

  1. Open the Dashboard. Skim Recent Activity and My Tasks.
  2. Open Tasks, click Sync All Sources, and see what's due.
  3. Glance at the Sales Pipeline to see if any deal needs a nudge.

Common jobs → where to go

  • Log a payment → Members → open the person → Payment History → Record Payment.
  • Record a promised payment → Members → open the person → Record Commitment.
  • Start working a big deal → Members → open the person → Track Major Deal → it appears in Sales Pipeline.
  • Find lapsed customers to win back → Members → turn on the Lapsed Members filter.
  • After a networking meeting → Voice Notes → upload/record → review auto-created contacts & next steps.
  • Court a new partner org → Companies → add it → move it along the stages.
  • Write a proposal or grant → Content Repository (copy proven text) + a Projects board to track it.
  • Invite people to an event → Events (create it) → Email Marketing (send the invite).
How it connects: Members is the hub. Payments flow from Members into the Sales Pipeline. Follow-ups from Members, Voice Notes, and Projects all roll up into Tasks. Events and Email Marketing reach back out to the same people. Keep Members accurate and everything else stays trustworthy.

Glossary

Every term in this guide, in plain English.

CRM
Customer Relationship Management — the system itself. One home for people, money, work, and outreach.
Member
Any person in the CRM: customer, prospect, supporter.
Where: Members
Payment
Money actually received from a member.
Where: 360° view → Record Payment
Commitment
Money promised now, to be paid later.
Where: 360° view → Record Commitment
Deal / Major Deal
A sale you're actively working, tracked through stages.
Where: Track Major Deal → Sales Pipeline
Lifetime Value (LTV)
The total a member has ever paid you.
Where: on each Member card
Lapsed Member
Someone who bought last year but nothing yet this year — a win-back target.
Where: Members → Lapsed Members filter
Sales Pipeline
The collection of deals and the stages they move through.
Where: Sales Pipeline
Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)
Roughly what it costs to win each deal.
Weighted value
A deal's dollar amount × its probability of closing — a realistic forecast.
Company
An organization you're courting as a partner/account.
Where: Companies
Member Client
An account already set up on the CRM (tier, status, setup).
Where: Admin → Member Clients
Contact
A non-customer person (vendor, partner, advisor) in the directory.
Where: Contacts
Board (Kanban)
A visual project layout where cards move across columns as work progresses.
Where: Projects
Grant
Grant work you manage — proposals and grant applications are supported alongside sales.
Where: Content Repository, linked in the 360° view
Semantic Search
Search by meaning rather than exact words.

Stripe
The payment processor you connect to auto-sync online payments and subscriptions.
